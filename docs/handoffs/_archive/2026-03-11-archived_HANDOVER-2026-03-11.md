## What Worked
- Cross-referencing Instagram and TikTok data to find consistent patterns (cap/outdoor video = best on both platforms)
- The 6-second-hook skill framework applied to the 0:01 retention drop diagnosis
- Identifying the aerial train B-roll video as the breakout format — no face, cinematic footage = 2x views and 10x follower conversion vs talking heads

## What Didn't Work (and Fixes)
- Talking-head videos (all formats) → average watch time 1–2 seconds, all drop at 0:01; fixed by pivoting to voiceover-over-train-footage format
- Longer videos (23 seconds on Instagram) → worst skip rate of all tested content; fixed by targeting 8–10 second maximum
- "Free link in bio" framing → below-average performance; removed from all scripts
- Initial 15–25 second scripts were too long given the 0:01 drop-off data → revised down to 10-second voiceover scripts

## Key Decisions
- **Voiceover over B-roll, not talking head**: The aerial train footage video is the only one that broke out of the ~150-view test batch. All future content should use train footage as the primary visual.
- **10 seconds max, not 15–25**: Original brief was 15–25s but retention data showed average watch time of 1–2 seconds across all videos. Shorter is better until retention improves.
- **TikTok captions include keywords in body**: Unlike Instagram (hashtags in first comment), TikTok search indexes caption text — "train identification" and "LocoSnap" added to all TikTok captions.
- **Keepalive ping via curl to Upstash REST API**: Backend Render URL was unknown; used Upstash REST API directly (`/ping` endpoint) instead of `/api/health`.

## Lessons Learned & Gotchas
- The single biggest lever is the **visual in frame 0** — not the words. Train footage stops the scroll; a person's face does not (for this niche).
- "Most viewers stopped watching at 0:01" on every TikTok video = the hook must work before the first word is spoken.
- 97.4% FYP traffic means zero loyal audience yet — the algorithm is doing its job but content isn't giving it enough signal to push wider. Target: 70%+ past second 6.
- Search query "pegasus train app" = 49.9% of search traffic → a direct competitor exists; consider a "vs Pegasus" or "if you've heard of Pegasus..." video.
- "How to freese snap loco" search query = users already know LocoSnap and are searching for it. Name recognition exists, just needs to be capitalised on.
