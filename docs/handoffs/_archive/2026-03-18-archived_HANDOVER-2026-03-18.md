## What Worked
- `cropdetect` filter in FFmpeg to precisely locate content within a black-bar'd video (`crop=1072:944:4:608` = content starts at y=608)
- Blur-extend approach: take top of image content, scale to fill black area, heavy blur, overlay — produced seamless-looking sky extension in one pass
- The "Sad Notebook" script landed well in `0318.mp4` — captions "years of blurry handwriting smudged by biro... pages soaked in rain... then they built LocoSnap" tell the story effectively
- Deadpan storytelling angle outperforms direct "download the app" content (confirmed by Instagram skip rate data — dev story reel had better-than-typical retention)

## What Didn't Work (and Fixes)
- **CapCut avatar image creator on cartoon faces**: Doesn't animate mouth properly for non-photorealistic images → Fix: use HeyGen Photo Avatar (manual face region marking) or D-ID for illustrated characters
- **Views not converting to testers**: Posts got reach (217 TikTok, 129+85 Instagram) but zero sign-ups → Fix: direct DM outreach to likers/commenters rather than waiting for organic CTA clicks
- **Seam at blur-extend junction**: Slight visible hard edge where blurred fill meets real image in `0318_fixed.mp4` → Acceptable at phone viewing distance/playback speed; could be improved with gradient feather if needed

## Lessons Learned & Gotchas
- **Gmail is the #1 tester drop-off point**: When DMing potential testers, ask for their Gmail address immediately — don't let them attempt the opt-in first and hit the "App not available" wall
- **The male-skewed audience (83%) is correct for rail content** — don't try to broaden it, lean into it
- **Skip rate on "dev story" content (74.7%) is better than "product demo" content** — personal narrative hooks better than features-first for this audience
- **Blur-extend works best when the original image has sky/gradient at the top** — the 125's sunset backdrop made the extension look natural because blurred sky = sky
- **CapCut avatar image creator = human faces only** — save time, go straight to HeyGen/D-ID for cartoon/illustrated subjects
