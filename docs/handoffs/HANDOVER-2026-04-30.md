## Lessons Learned & Gotchas

- **Day 2 > Day 1 on Android** is the headline. Don't read it as anomaly — first DE Pro Annual + first FR + first BT Play purchases all visible in RevenueCat = expansion phase, not launch decay. Watch this trend over 7 days before reading the Apr 29 drop-off as significant.
- **Polish surge is bi-platform now.** iOS already proved it; Android Day 2 numbers (4 PL of 20 = 20%) confirm it carries through to the Play Store funnel. Polish content investment is structural, not platform-bound.
- **IG retention is structural, not topical.** The same exact EN57 video pulled 7× more views on TikTok. Different audience, different consumption pattern. The IG pre-roll card on tomorrow's ad is the first deliberate test of whether topic-clarity is the lever; if not, the next experiment should be format-restructuring (slower opens, longer beats) or platform-specific re-shoots.
- **Anthropic Sonnet caching is the dominant cost lever.** 33M tokens at 59.1% read ratio = the fix that pays the monthly bill. Haiku is much smaller (9M tokens) so even if the post-deploy ratio reaches 50% the savings are smaller in absolute terms (~$3-4/week vs Sonnet's much larger ongoing benefit).
- **First Lifetime Pro ask is a market-research moment, not a build trigger.** Default reply pattern: turn ask into price discovery. Each unanswered ask = wasted signal.
- **608×1080 portrait is increasingly common Polish-source footage** — many PL trainspotters shoot at this resolution rather than 1080×1920. Aspect within 0.1% of 9:16 so simple scale works; sharpening pass optional. Worth normalising for future PL ad workflows.
- **Brainstorming skill applies to creative production** even when the output is a video, not code. The 6-question flow caught the Pro-tier-gating leaderboard decision yesterday and caught the "Diese Lok kennt jeder" hook today. Process discipline > shortcuts.
