## Lessons Learned & Gotchas

- **Long-form commenter corrections are high-value** — One German rail enthusiast corrected TWO videos today (the ICE 1 that was actually an ICE 2, and the ICE L that showed the Steuerwagen). Both corrections were technically sophisticated — they identified the BR 402 from the Schaku flap cover design and knew about the future BR 105 matching the Steuerwagen. These commenters are the exact audience LocoSnap wants. Responses that specifically credit their expertise and promise follow-through convert them from critics into advocates.
- **The 0:02 drop-off is universal across both languages and platforms** — Frankfurt DE, Frankfurt EN, and Frankfurt IG DE all show "most viewers stopped watching at 0:02". The text-based quiz hook ("WAS IST DAS?") doesn't beat this. The BR 101 pattern-interrupt hook is the next test.
- **Per-video geography self-corrects faster than account-wide geography** — Account is still 72.4% Poland, but individual German-targeted videos reach 62-83% Germany. The account-wide number is a lagging indicator; it doesn't matter for download conversion.
- **German rail enthusiasts fact-check via specific visual details** — Not just class numbers. The Schaku flap cover difference between ICE 1 and ICE 2 is the kind of detail serious spotters use — this is more reliable than formation length when the photo is a front shot.
- **The app may have no scan timeout** — The first screen recording today showed the app stuck in "Identifying..." forever after a scan request apparently failed during an LTE-to-WiFi handover. Worth investigating in a future backend session — there may be no timeout/retry/error state for failed scan requests.
- **iPhone status bar colour on screen recordings changes with time** — The first (failed) and second (good) screen recordings were from adjacent timestamps but showed different network indicators. Extract frames at multiple timestamps when reviewing any screen recording to catch state changes.
- **Brainstorming skill terminal state is writing-plans**, not implementation. Followed correctly this session — the brainstorm produced a design doc which was committed before any video encoding work started.
