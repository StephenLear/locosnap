## Lessons Learned & Gotchas
- **`public.spots` SELECT was `USING (true)`** — a latent location exposure (lat/lng world-readable). Fixed in 018. **When tightening a base-table RLS, audit `security_invoker` on dependent views**: migration 011 made the 4 leaderboard views `security_invoker = on`, so they relied on the permissive policy. Post-018 they are DEFINER — **future migrations must keep them definer and NEVER add lat/lng or raw spot columns** (would re-leak location).
- **`leaderboard_rarity` view exposes only rare/epic/legendary counts** (no uncommon). The explainer + displayed score both use exactly those three — an invariant comment was added at the `computeRarityScore` call in `CollectionTab.tsx`; do NOT add `uncommonCount` or the explainer total desyncs from the row.
- **DE owns Android (~62% MAU), the PL surge is iOS-only** — don't read "Poland overtaking Germany" (iOS units, small-N) as the overall market. Platform-split targeting.
- **Lifetime heavy-user margin risk is now REAL** — the first lifetime buyer is a 60+/hr power scanner; lifetime nets ~€63-76, breaks even ~1,500 scans at ~$0.06 marginal. The 60/hr cap bounds worst-case burn; do not reflexively raise it.
- **`eas submit` uploads the binary only** — release notes + submit-for-review (iOS) / notes + rollout (Android) are separate manual store-console steps.
- **Haul-ad beats must SHOW THE SCAN, not the detail page.** First DE cut used the static results/detail card for the opener → user: "rubbish, only has the details page and not a scan." The scan recordings contain a scan-reticle phase (green targeting sweep over the train) BEFORE the reveal card — every beat must open on that, then cut to the reveal. Some cards (SU 46) scroll to specs ~1s after load, so freeze the reveal card as a still for the held portion while keeping the scan portion live.
- **Two annual endcards are UNTRACKED in git** (`ad_assets/endcards/endcard_de_annual.mp4`, `endcard_pl_annual.mp4`) — content assets, not build-critical, but commit them if you want them preserved in the repo alongside the other endcards.
