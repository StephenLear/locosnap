### 3. Break-even analysis
Costs ~$300-375/month (Anthropic API ~$295 dominates; caching maxed at 97.9%). Net revenue ~$35-85/month. Break-even ≈ 120-140 paying subscribers (currently ~12). Blueprint generation cost confirmed ≈ $0 (Pro-only, opt-in, not per-scan).

### 4. Subscriber-growth research brief
Deep research via 3 parallel agents. Key finding: the ~2% free-to-paid conversion is the freemium median — not broken; the problem is the cohort. Of 374 registered users: 137 (37%) never scanned, 125 (33%) did 1-5 scans, 112 (30%) hit the 6-scan wall. The 112 wall-hitters are the prime conversion pool. A Day-1 retention cliff is flagged as a separate, larger gate. The Apple "Average Retention" chart is unreliable (non-monotonic; opt-in only) — `apple_stats.md`'s old retention figures are not trustworthy.

### 5. Monetisation brainstorm → v1.0.35 design + plan
Landed the "Club" model: free 3-scan taster → €1 one-time club membership (no ads, no data selling, ever) → Pro subscription (Annual hero). Existing free users not grandfathered. Member scan allowance ~4/week. Approach B placement (tier-aware home card + peak-moment auto-open paywall). Review-readiness baked in.

## What Worked
- 3 parallel research agents kept the main context clean while producing a well-sourced brief.
- The brainstorm caught two genuine cost errors the user raised (a one-time €1 cannot fund ongoing scans; grandfathering = a free ongoing-cost giveaway) — the back-and-forth produced a better model than the first draft.
- Pulling the live `wrong_id_reports` table and the Supabase user distribution grounded the strategy in real numbers.

## What Didn't Work (and Fixes)
- Could not pull PostHog cohort retention — the Claude-in-Chrome automation tab runs in a separate session from the user's browser login, and the PostHog account was brand-new/unused. Fix: the retention number is not needed to decide the €1 model; get a clean cohort read later from Play Console or PostHog once it has data.
- The Apple retention chart was initially over-read as "retention catastrophically broken" — it is non-monotonic and opt-in-only, so unreliable.

## Key Decisions
- v1.0.35 = the "Club" monetisation model. The €1 is a one-time membership — not a subscription intro, and not an unlimited-scans unlock (a one-time payment cannot fund an ongoing per-use cost).
- No ads — decided against. The €1 *guarantees* no ads rather than removing them.
- Do not grandfather existing free users — convert them via the €1-join offer.
- Retire the "€1 first month of Pro" intro — only one €1 in the app (the membership).
- This monetisation work is the *conversion* layer; the Day-1 retention cliff is a separate, also-needed stream.

## Lessons Learned & Gotchas
- A one-time payment cannot fund an ongoing per-use cost — unlimited scans must be subscription-funded.
- Corrective/pedantic comments on ads are net-positive (algorithm distribution) — do not frame them as a problem (saved as memory `feedback_ad_comment_corrections_positive.md`).
- The Claude-in-Chrome MCP tab does not share the user's logged-in browser session — it cannot read the user's authenticated dashboards; the user must screenshot.
- EAS build credits are at ~95-100% — pay-as-you-go for any further build this cycle.
